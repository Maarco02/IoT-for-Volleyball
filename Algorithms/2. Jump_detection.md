**1. Treshold based**
   Identify three tresholds that helps to detect the three phases of a jump:
   1. Jump's load: acceleration decrease when the subject flexes the knees
   2. Flight: values close to zero, needs to declare a minimum time to consider it as a jump and cut off errors and other movements
   3. Landing: if the acceleration is >g means that the jump's ended

   the accelerometers gives the three axis values, so to make analysis, the absolute acceleration has to be calculated as $$A_tot = sqrt(ax^2 + ay^2 + az^2)$$
   (check if the data gives the normalized value with respect to g or the nominal value of the acceleration in $$\frac{m^2}{s}$$).

**2. NDI method (Numerical Double Integration)**  
   The jump heights are calculated through a double integration over the time
   Given the acceleration from the sensor, we do two integration using the trapezoidal rule:
   1. first integration to pass from the vertical acceleration to the velocity
   2. second integration to pass from the velocity to the position

   The two integration are done for each jump, by considering v(0)=0 at the instant when the athlete starts to load the jump, the time interval stops when the athlete lands
   The Trapezoidal Rule is used to improve the precision by reducing the error due to truncation done by the rectangular integration(Euler)

**3. TOV Take Off Velocity**
   The method ignores the air resistance, and takes into account the height recahed by the center of mass, the related formula is 
      $$H_{TOV} = \frac{v^{2}_{TO}}{2g}+S_{TO}$$  where
         $$v_{TO}$$ is the take off veloccity, obtained integrating the filtered acceleration measured before the take of instant;
         $$S_{TO}$$ is the vertical displacement, or the height reached during the phase of jump when the feet are still on the ground;
         The take off instant is defined as the instant when the athlete leave the floor and the vertical acceleration is g.

**4. FT Time of Flight**
   Considering the athlet as a particel in free fall conditions, so we assume that the CoM follows a perfeclty simmetric parabolic trajectory
      $$H_{FT} = S_{TO} + |\frac{1}{2}(-g)(\frac{t_{f}}{2})^2|$$ where
      t_{f} is the time of flight calculated from the take off instant to the landing instant.
